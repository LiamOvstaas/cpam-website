Core Inspection & Reliability Services

Mill Girth Gear Inspection

  • Conducting third-party, non-destructive inspections of mill girth gear teeth as per ASTM E2905 to identify macro-pitting, spalling, wear (abrasive/adhesive), scuffing, deformation, indentations, and cracking.
  • Offering alternative advanced NDT methods on request to locate and size subsurface fatigue cracking or manufacturing defects.
  • Performing real-time analysis during scanning to identify and characterize defect indications.
  • Executes targeted crack relief (excavation) as required, based on inspection findings within the defined maintenance scope.
  • Completes cleaning, using Cleansolv™ products and inspection in a single maintenance window to minimize operational disruption.
  • Assessing observed failure modes using ANSI/AGMA 1010-F14, including severity and potential operational impact (clean gear set required).
  • Provides dynamic inspection during operation and prior to cleaning, including thermal imaging and dynamic photography of the gear and pinion.
  • Delivers a technical report for maintenance planning and insurance risk assessment, including findings, defect locations, severity levels, and recommendations for monitoring or repair.

Mill Shell, Heads & Trunnions Mapping

  • Performs systematic Ultrasonic Thickness (UT) mapping to identify minimum remaining wall thickness.
  • Uses a structured grid inspection with a reference point for consistent and repeatable coverage.
  • Scans full areas within each defined grid cell and records minimum thickness values for each location.
  • Delivers a thickness map and tabulated dataset, including color-scaled visualization to clearly identify areas of concern.
  • Supports maintenance planning and liner strategy review with data that enables risk-based decision-making and helps avoid unplanned shell or head repairs.

Plant-Wide Piping Surveys

  • Performs third-party piping condition surveys to support replacement planning and risk-based management of plant and tailings piping systems.
  • Aligns survey approach with applicable integrity and design standards and site programs.
  • Assesses piping, pressure vessel, and weld integrity using field-proven methods and industry standards to identify potential failures and reduce unscheduled downtime.
  • Delivers clear outputs to enable planning and reduce unplanned leaks or outages.
  • Extensive field experience in piping, pressure vessel, and weld integrity assessment.
